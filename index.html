Unveiling the Metropolis: About Dubai

Dubai, a dazzling jewel in the Middle East, has transformed from a humble pearling and fishing settlement into a global metropolis renowned for its audacious architecture, luxury, and groundbreaking advancements.

The discovery of oil in the late 1960s accelerated its growth, but Dubai brilliantly diversified into tourism, finance, trade, and technology, becoming a beacon of innovation and a strategic hub for international business and leisure.

From bustling souks to serene beaches, Dubai captivates with its dynamic blend of futuristic vision and rich cultural heritage.

Dubai's population is approximately **3.6 million** (as of 2025), a vibrant melting pot of over 200 nationalities, with expatriates making up about 85%.

Dubai is strategically located on the southeast coast of the Persian Gulf, at **25.2°N, 55.3°E**, serving as a pivotal hub for global trade and travel.

Dubai experiences a hot desert climate with summer temperatures of **35–45°C** (95–113°F) and milder winters of **20–25°C** (68–77°F). Rainfall is scarce, occurring mainly in winter.

Embrace the Rich Heritage

Delve into Dubai's authentic soul by exploring its rich cultural heritage through traditional souks, immersive museums, and historic districts like Al Fahidi.

Emirati culture is deeply rooted in Bedouin traditions, celebrated through art, music, falconry, camel racing, and an enduring spirit of warm hospitality that welcomes every visitor.
